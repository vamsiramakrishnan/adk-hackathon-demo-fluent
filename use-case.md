Use Case 4: Telecommunications
Project Title: The Proactive Network Outage Communication Agent
An intelligent, multi-agent system that transforms our telco company’s network outage response
from reactive chaos into proactive, high-touch customer communication, safeguarding our most
valuable accounts.

The Business Problem
● The Pain Point: When a network outage occurs, we are immediately on the back foot. A
flood of angry support calls overwhelms our service centers, brand reputation plummets
on social media, and—most critically—our high-value enterprise clients begin starting the
clock on costly SLA violations. Our current response is slow, manual, and fails to
prioritize the customers who matter most.
● The C-Suite Objective: We must reduce customer churn by 15% and lower inbound call
center volume by 30% during network incidents. We need to be the first to notify our
customers, not the other way around.
The AI Agent Solution
● High-Level Agent Architecture: The Proactive Network Outage Communication Agent
is an automated response system that activates the instant a critical network fault is
detected. It simultaneously analyzes the technical problem and identifies the business
impact, orchestrating a targeted communication strategy in minutes, not hours.
● Data Sources (find or generate):
○ Network alerts (monitoring system log)
○ Customer accounts database
● Considerations:
○ Solution itself should be robust to being affected by the outage
○ Outage Response Coordinator: A critical alert from our network alerts
monitoring system..
○ Network Analyst: This agent immediately parses the complex, technical
network alerts logs. It identifies the root cause of the fault (e.g., "fiber cut,"
"routing failure"), determines the precise geographic blast radius, and estimates a
preliminary time-to-resolution (ETR).
○ Customer Impact Analyst: This agent queries the Customer Accounts
Database. It cross-references the outage's blast radius with customer locations
to identify all affected parties. Crucially, it stratifies this list, flagging high-value
accounts: enterprise clients with SLAs, government agencies, and VIP residential
customers.
○ Decision & Action: Craft distinct, targeted communications:

■ For Enterprise Clients: A formal, technical notification detailing the issue
and referencing their SLA.
■ For VIP Residential Customers: A simplified, empathetic message via
SMS or email with the ETR.
These drafts are then routed to a human communications manager for a
final one-click approval before dissemination.
